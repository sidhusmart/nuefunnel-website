## The situation

A consumer e-commerce operations team was spending half a day every Monday assembling a weekly performance report. The numbers came from a Snowflake warehouse that the data team owned, accessed via SQL that the ops team did not write. Every question outside the standard report — "what did returns look like for the new line last week?" — became a Slack request to a data analyst, who got to it on their own timeline. Decisions were being made on stale numbers.

## What we built

A natural-language interface over their existing Snowflake warehouse:

- Plain-English question in, SQL generation out, executed against a constrained read-only role.
- Schema-aware: the system knew the actual table layout and column semantics, not just the column names.
- A safety layer that refused queries it wasn't confident about, rather than hallucinating numbers.
- Results rendered as small inline charts or tables, with the generated SQL viewable for anyone who wanted to verify.

Zero data migration. Zero new data platform. We worked on top of the warehouse the data team already maintained.

## Result

- The Monday reporting cycle disappeared entirely. The standard numbers are pulled on demand.
- The ops team asks roughly **15 ad-hoc questions per day** of the interface, replacing what used to be Slack tickets.
- The data analysts now spend their time on actual data work — pipeline design, schema decisions — not on running queries for other teams.

## Scope and shape

6 weeks, 2 people. We worked closely with the head of ops and the data lead — the data lead's involvement was essential, because the safety layer needed real opinions about which queries to refuse.
