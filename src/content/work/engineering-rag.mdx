## The situation

A 40-person engineering organization was bringing on five new hires per quarter. Every new engineer required two to three days of pair programming with a senior, primarily to absorb tacit knowledge that lived in Slack threads, half-finished Notion pages, and the heads of three specific people. The seniors were burning out on it. The team wanted onboarding to feel like supervised exploration, not extraction.

## What we built

A retrieval-augmented chat interface, indexed against their existing knowledge corpus:

- Notion workspace (architecture docs, RFC archive, onboarding guides).
- GitHub repos (READMEs, CONTRIBUTING files, recent PR descriptions).
- A curated subset of Slack threads, filtered to channels with substantive technical discussion.

The interface was deliberately plain: a chat box, a citations sidebar that linked back to source documents, and a "tag a senior" button when the new hire wanted human input. No new docs platform, no migration project.

## Result

- New hires now reach productive first commit in **about six hours** of supervised exploration, down from two-to-three days of pair programming.
- The senior engineers report spending **roughly 70% less time** on onboarding-shaped questions.
- The citation sidebar surfaces stale documents — the team uses it as a passive doc-health signal.

## Scope and shape

4 weeks, 2 people, coding-agent-assisted for the indexing and retrieval layer. The team owns the running system; we transferred the infrastructure-as-code at the end of the engagement.
