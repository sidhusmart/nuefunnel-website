## The situation

A regional consumer fintech was scaling faster than their support team could hire. Their customers preferred WhatsApp over email or phone, and most incoming tickets fell into a small set of recurring patterns: balance checks, card-block requests, transaction disputes, and KYC status. The team wanted to handle the predictable 60% with an LLM-driven assistant and free up their human agents for the actual edge cases.

## What we built

A WhatsApp Business API integration with a small, well-scoped agent that:

- Authenticates the user against the existing fintech CRM via structured tool calls.
- Handles balance, recent transactions, and card-status queries inline (no human handoff).
- Escalates anything ambiguous or compliance-sensitive to a human agent with full conversation context attached.
- Logs every interaction to the same ticketing system the human team already used, so reporting stays unified.

We deliberately did not introduce a new system of record. The agent reads from and writes to their existing infrastructure — no new database, no parallel CRM.

## Result

Within four weeks of rollout:

- **60% of first-touch tickets** are now resolved by the assistant.
- **Median response time** dropped from 14 minutes to under 30 seconds for the deflected segment.
- The support team shifted to a tiered model — three agents handle the residual 40%, instead of seven handling everything.

## Scope and shape

5 weeks, 2 people, substantial use of coding agents for the integration layer. We worked directly with their CTO and head of support — no project manager in between.
