Whatsapp and messengers like Telegram are some of the most popular forms of communication between people. It makes sense that businesses would also like to use these platforms to communicate with their customers. As LLMs increasingly take over the mundane aspects of customer support, we are seeing the integration of LLMs into the customer service process. We enabled such a solution for one of our clients and we discuss some of the unique challenges that we solved in the process.

While chat continues to be the most popular form of interaction between humans, when it comes to interacting with a bot we often see a reluctance in typing. Customers often prefer to interact with simpler clicks rather than type long messages explaining the issue. So even though the bot lives within a chat interface, it is often helpful to provide the user simpler buttons and easier responses as a way to navigate. We do this with the help of buttons in Whatsapp but each button is generated dynamically via the LLM structured response.
